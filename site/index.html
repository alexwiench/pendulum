What it does

A curve editor that lives where you already work.

Pendulum sits in an After Effects panel. No round-tripping to the graph editor, no copy/paste math from Stack Overflow.

Drag to design

Grab a control point, drag it, watch your animation update live. Overshoot, anticipate, or glide — it's all in the curve.

Preset library

Save any curve as a named preset with its own color tag. Build a personal library or share one across a team.

One-click apply

Select keyframes, click a preset, done. Pendulum rewrites the keyframe interpolation for you in a single undoable step.

Reads any keyframes

Drop in on existing keyframes and Pendulum infers the curve — tweak it, save it, keep moving.

Grid of thumbnails

Your presets live as tiny, color-tagged curve previews so you can spot the one you want at a glance.

Non-destructive

Pendulum never modifies layers beyond the keyframes you apply it to. Undo works like you'd expect.

Opening the panel

After installing, launch After Effects and choose Window → Pendulum.jsx from the menu bar. The panel will dock into your workspace like any other. We recommend parking it next to your Timeline — it's where you'll spend most of your time with it.

Drawing your first curve

The graph shows a speed curve — velocity over time. Under the hood it's a cubic-bezier(x1, 0, x2, 1) position curve, and Pendulum plots its derivative so you can see the shape of the motion's pacing.

  1. Drag the orange handles along the baseline. The left handle controls influence in (how long the move takes to get going), the right one controls influence out (how long it takes to settle).
  2. Watch the preview. The glowing dot sliding along the bottom rail is the actual animated object — its position follows the cubic-bezier.
  3. Copy the bezier string from the readout if you want to paste it into a CSS easing or AE expression.
A tall narrow peak = a fast, snappy move. A wide gentle hump = a slow, smooth move. Two peaks (via an expression-driven curve) give you a bounce.

Applying to keyframes

Pendulum applies your curve to the interpolation between two adjacent keyframes.

  1. Select two or more adjacent keyframes in the timeline.
  2. Shape your curve in the Pendulum panel.
  3. Click Apply (or press ⏎).

Pendulum rewrites the bezier handles on the selected keyframes in one undoable step. Press ⌘Z / Ctrl+Z to revert if you change your mind.

Saving a preset

Once you have a curve you like, click the + button in the preset bar. Name the preset, pick a color tag, save. It now lives in your library forever — across projects, across sessions.

Color tags

Each preset gets a color. Use them however you like — by project, by intensity, by mood. Pendulum ships with a sensible default palette:

  • Blue — neutral / default
  • Purple — custom / bespoke
  • Red — ease-in shapes
  • Green — ease-out shapes
  • Orange — aggressive / snappy

Managing your library

Right-click any preset to rename, re-tag, duplicate, or delete it. Drag presets around to reorder them. Your library is stored in your AE preferences folder and syncs with any cloud-synced preferences you already have set up.

Keyboard shortcuts

  • ⏎ — apply current curve to selected keyframes
  • ⌥ + drag handle — fine-tune (10× slower)
  • ⇧ + drag handle — constrain to axis
  • R — reset to linear
  • 1–9 — load preset 1–9

Tips & tricks

Double-click a preset to both load and apply in a single action — great for speed-keyframing a whole sequence.

Hold ⌥ when clicking a preset to apply it without loading it into the editor — useful when you've already got a curve you're working on and don't want to lose it.

FAQ

Questions people ask.

Which versions of After Effects are supported?
Pendulum is tested on After Effects 2022 through the current release. It's a ScriptUI panel, so anything that supports .jsx panels should work — but older CC versions aren't officially supported.
Is it really free?
Yep. Pendulum is MIT-licensed and free forever. If it saves you time, starring the repo helps more people find it.
Can I share my preset library with teammates?
Yes. Your library is a single JSON file. Export it from the panel menu, drop it in Dropbox / a shared folder, and teammates can import it on their end.
Does it work with expressions or only keyframes?
Pendulum applies to keyframe interpolation. If you need a curve inside an expression, copy the cubic-bezier() readout from the editor and paste the four numbers into your own bezier() expression.
I found a bug / want a feature.
Open an issue on GitHub. Pendulum is actively maintained and PRs are welcome.
Why is it called Pendulum?
Good motion has swing — a sense of weight, of easing in and out of rest. A pendulum is the simplest system that has it.
